# Philosophy

myalicia is one possible answer to a design question: what does it look like to build software that takes the shape of a single human, and grows with them?

## Humorphism, in one paragraph

[Humorphism](https://humorphism.com) is the design philosophy that technology should take a shape that fits the human, not the other way around. It's the ergonomic principle applied to AI: stop building chatbots that everyone has to learn to talk to, start designing relationships that learn the person they're with. Where skeuomorphism once made digital things look like physical things so we could approach them, humorphism makes AI things *respond* like attentive presences so we can be in relationship with them.

## From chatbot to teammate

Most AI products today are tools you adapt to. You learn the prompt that gets the result. You phrase your question in the way the model parses cleanly. You contort.

A teammate doesn't ask you to contort. A teammate notices how you work, picks up your idiom, remembers what you said yesterday, and catches the patterns you can't see in yourself. A teammate has different conversations with different people, and gets better at one relationship over time, not worse.

myalicia is what the architecture of that looks like, made buildable.

## Why three loops

Most agents only have one loop: the conversation. You speak, they answer, the moment ends. There's no metabolism — nothing that turns today's exchange into tomorrow's understanding.

A relationship needs three depths of attention to work:

- **Listen** — being present in the moment
- **Notice** — catching patterns across moments
- **Know** — coming to know the person, over time

Each loop reads from and writes to a shared substrate (your vault, your notes, your archetype). Awareness compounds because three different cadences of reflection are always running. That compounding is what makes a teammate, not a tool.

## Knowledge in altitudes

Inputs become synthesis. Synthesis becomes wisdom. The substrate doesn't store flat — it metabolizes upward. Notes you took at 1pm become connections at midnight. Connections from this week become character traits across months. External research keeps the loop open, so the system isn't echoing only itself.

This is what your vault becomes when an attentive system lives inside it.

## Self as a teammate quality

A teammate who fixes themselves when they break, who learns from the relationship, who notices their own gaps — that's the difference between a tool and a partner. myalicia is built to be:

- **Self-healing** — when a skill errors, she notices, logs the trajectory, and proposes a fix in the next reflection
- **Self-extending** — she can author new skills based on observed gaps in her own behavior
- **Self-aware** — every action runs through metacognitive confidence assessment; uncertain actions escalate to deeper models

These aren't internal plumbing — they're presented to the user as teammate qualities, because that's what they are.

## What we ask of designers

The agentic era asks designers to think differently. Stop designing interfaces. Start designing relationships. Stop designing chatbots. Start designing the metabolic loops by which an AI comes to know someone.

Concretely: contribute **awareness primitives**. A reading-pattern primitive. A calendar-rhythm primitive. A relationship-graph primitive. Each one is a small, opinionated module that turns some slice of life into structured memory the loops can metabolize. That's the work of design now.

## What we ask of builders

Read the code. Notice how thin the harness is and how fat the skills are. That's by design — the small core stays out of the way so the skills can be where the personality lives. Add a skill, share it, fork the project for your own use.

The goal is not one Alicia served to everyone. The goal is many myalicia instances, each shaped to one person, each growing in its own direction.

That's humorphism in code form.
